  • Braces (Orthodontics)

    This specialty involves treating malocclusions, otherwise known as improper bites caused due to irregular teeth or uneven jaw alignment or even both. In Greek, ‘ortho’ means to correct and ‘odons’ means tooth.

    Why is it Required?:
    Orthodontic treatment (or braces) can be provided purely aesthetic reasons, i.e. to improve a person’s appearance of teeth and smile. Treatment is most often prescribed for practical reasons such as providing an occlusion or an improved bite.

    ‘Crowding’ of Teeth: A common reason why orthodontic treatment is absolutely required, is due to ‘crowding’ of teeth—a situation where there is no room for the normal adult teeth. This may require tooth removal in order to make enough room for the remaining teeth.

    Types of Braces:
    The most common are the traditional metal braces made of stainless steel (sometimes in combination with nickel titanium).
    Clear braces are a more cosmetic alternative to traditional metal braces. They are almost transparent and blend in with the teeth’s natural colour. These brackets are made of ceramic or plastic alloys, and clear elastic and white metal ties are used with clear braces to help keep them less noticeable. However, these tend to be more brittle than the metal braces, and can fracture easily.
    Gold-plated stainless-steel braces are for those who are allergic to nickel. Also, they tend to blend better with teeth, plus some prefer the look of gold over silver-coloured braces.
    Progressive clear retainers may be used to gradually move teeth into their final position. These have the same effect as braces but can be removed easily for eating.
