  • Braces (Orthodontics)

    This specialty involves treating malocclusions, otherwise known as improper bites caused due to irregular teeth or uneven jaw alignment or even both. In Greek, ‘ortho’ means to correct and ‘odons’ means tooth.

    Why is it Required?:
    Orthodontic treatment (or braces) can be provided purely aesthetic reasons, i.e. to improve a person’s appearance of teeth and smile. Treatment is most often prescribed for practical reasons such as providing an occlusion or an improved bite.

    ‘Crowding’ of Teeth: A common reason why orthodontic treatment is absolutely required, is due to ‘crowding’ of teeth—a situation where there is no room for the normal adult teeth. This may require tooth removal in order to make enough room for the remaining teeth.

    Types of Braces:
    The most common are the traditional metal braces made of stainless steel (sometimes in combination with nickel titanium).
    Clear braces are a more cosmetic alternative to traditional metal braces. They are almost transparent and blend in with the teeth’s natural colour. These brackets are made of ceramic or plastic alloys, and clear elastic and white metal ties are used with clear braces to help keep them less noticeable. However, these tend to be more brittle than the metal braces, and can fracture easily.
    Gold-plated stainless-steel braces are for those who are allergic to nickel. Also, they tend to blend better with teeth, plus some prefer the look of gold over silver-coloured braces.
    Progressive clear retainers may be used to gradually move teeth into their final position. These have the same effect as braces but can be removed easily for eating.

  • Restorative Dentistry

    Dental Fillings:
    Dental fillings are used to restore carious teeth after removal of the dental decay (cavities) and help to strengthen and reinforce the remaining tooth structure. The filling materials used are - Composite Resin (tooth coloured) and amalgam (silver filling) and Dr. Suvarna will help you select the filling that is right for your needs.

    Dental Crowns:
    Dental crowns are ‘caps’ that fit onto your teeth. If a tooth is extremely broken down or restored or root canal treated, a dental crown reinforces the tooth by covering the remaining tooth structure. Crowns can be fabricated from different materials and can be made to look natural yet feel comfortable especially in the smile zone.

    Preventive Dental Care and Oral Hygiene:
    Regular professional dental cleaning helps maintain your oral hygiene and prevent gum disease. Dental plaque and calculus (hardened plaque) which cause gum disease is removed by a procedure called scaling and polishing. In molars with deep grooves that retain food debris, sealants can be placed to prevent development of cavities.

  • Root Canal Treatment (Endodontics)

    In Greek, ‘endo’ means inside, and ‘odons’ refer to teeth. This dentistry specialty means exactly that—the treatment of the ‘insides’ of your tooth. Endodontics refers to treating the diseased or injured tooth pulp, and the tissues that surround the root of your tooth. The tooth pulp consists of nerves as well as lymphatic and fibrous tissues. If this mass is infected or damaged, it is often unable to heal on its own. This is why, you will need endodontic treatment to save your tooth. Depending on the degree of infection or injury caused on your tooth, there are numerous endodontic treatments that we can provide. The most common one is called ‘root canal therapy’.

    This involves making an opening at the crown of the tooth to remove the damaged pulp, and the canals are then cleaned, enlarged, shaped and refilled. Sometimes after root canal treatment, you might still experience pain or discomfort, which could mean that the infection may still persist. In such cases, we conduct an ‘apicoectomy’—where the diseased tissue is removed from the bony area around the root of your tooth. We also perform other endodontic treatments including incision for drainage, and internal bleaching of the tooth that has blackened because of infiltration of decayed soft tissue. The latter is most often seen in incisors injured due to a sudden impact.
